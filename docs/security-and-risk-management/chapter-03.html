Chapter 3: Compliance

Introduction to Compliance​

  • Compliance in cybersecurity focuses on adhering to laws, regulations, industry standards, and contractual obligations.
  • Non-compliance can result in significant fines or business closure, making compliance a crucial part of an organization's security strategy.

Key Compliance Areas​

  1. Regulations, Laws, and Crimes Involving Computers

    • Laws protect various aspects of computer and information security, such as data privacy, computer misuse, software copyright, and data protection.
    • Compliance with these laws ensures that organizations protect intellectual property, maintain data privacy, and prevent fraud.

  2. Intellectual Property (IP) Protection

    • Copyright Law: Protects the creator’s rights over their original work, including software code, graphics, and literary works.
    • Copyright infringement is a growing issue, especially with the rise of peer-to-peer file-sharing technologies.

  3. Data Breaches and Privacy Laws

    • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): The strictest data privacy law, which mandates reporting a data breach within 72 hours.
    • HIPAA and PCI DSS are other critical laws that govern how organizations must handle sensitive data.

Compliance Requirements​

  • Organizations must implement specific controls and regularly assess their effectiveness to ensure compliance.
  • Key compliance frameworks include ISO 27001 for information security management and FedRAMP for cloud services used by the U.S. government.

Audits and Continuous Monitoring​

  • Audits: Internal or external reviews ensure the organization is following its compliance obligations.
    • They assess areas such as data protection, risk management, and adherence to privacy laws.
  • Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) programs: Integrate activities across governance, risk management, and compliance to ensure a holistic approach.

Investigations​

  1. Administrative Investigations: Focus on internal policy violations.
  2. Criminal Investigations: Aim to determine whether criminal activities have taken place.
  3. Civil Investigations: Often occur when lawsuits are imminent or ongoing.
  4. Regulatory Investigations: Government-led investigations to ensure the organization complies with relevant laws.

Reporting Requirements​

  • Different jurisdictions require organizations to notify authorities and affected individuals when a data breach occurs.
    • GDPR: Requires notification within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.
    • HIPAA: Allows up to 60 days for breach reporting.

Downstream Liability​

  • Organizations can be held liable for breaches or policy violations committed by third parties they work with.
    • Contracts with vendors should include security provisions and penalties for non-compliance.

Privacy and International Laws​

  • Compliance with global privacy laws, such as the GDPR and Brazil’s LGPD, is essential for organizations operating across borders.
  • International laws vary significantly, and organizations must coordinate with their legal teams to remain compliant across different jurisdictions.
